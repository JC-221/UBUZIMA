HIV/AIDS Awareness

Know HIV. Protect Yourself. Live Fully.

HIV (Human Immunodeficiency Virus) affects the immune system. With knowledge, testing, and treatment, people can live long healthy lives. UBUZIMA HUB helps you understand facts, prevention, and support.

  • ✔ Learn how HIV is transmitted
  • ✔ Understand prevention methods
  • ✔ Break stigma and discrimination
Take HIV Quiz

Key Facts

What is HIV?

HIV attacks the immune system and weakens the body's defense.

Transmission

Spread through unprotected sex, blood, or sharing needles.

Not Spread By

Hugging, sharing food, mosquito bites, or touching.

Treatment

ART helps people live long healthy lives (U=U concept).

Lessons

HIV weakens immune cells (CD4). Without treatment, it leads to AIDS. With ART medication, people can live normal lives.
Use condoms, test regularly, avoid sharing needles, and consider PrEP if at risk.
People on treatment can achieve undetectable viral load and cannot transmit HIV.

Take Mental Health Quiz

Key Facts

What is Mental Health?

It is emotional, psychological, and social well-being.

Common Issues

Stress, anxiety, depression, and trauma.

Warning Signs

Sadness, isolation, fatigue, or loss of interest.

Support is Important

Talking to someone is a sign of strength, not weakness.

Lessons

Mental health is about how we think, feel, and behave. Good mental health helps us handle stress and make decisions.
Stress is a reaction to pressure. Anxiety is long-term worry. Breathing exercises and talking help reduce it.
Depression is not weakness. It is a medical condition that can be treated.

Take Menstrual Quiz

Key Facts

What is Menstruation?

It is the monthly shedding of the uterine lining.

Cycle Length

Usually between 21 to 35 days.

Duration

Periods usually last 3 to 7 days.

Hygiene is Important

Proper hygiene prevents infections and discomfort.

Lessons

The menstrual cycle is controlled by hormones and prepares the body for pregnancy each month.
Use clean materials, change regularly, and maintain hygiene to avoid infections.
Periods are natural and should never be a source of shame or discrimination.

Key Facts

What are STIs?

Infections passed through sexual contact. Many are treatable.

Prevention

Use protection, test regularly, and stay informed.

Symptoms

Some STIs show no symptoms — testing is important.

Safety

Healthy relationships are based on respect and consent.

Lessons

STIs are infections caused by bacteria, viruses, or parasites. Some are curable, others can be managed with treatment.
Use protection, avoid risky behavior, and get regular health checks.
Respect, consent, communication, and trust are key to healthy relationships.
